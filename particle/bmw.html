BMW Careers

UX/UI Design (Desktop and Mobile)

Wireframes


The experience layout was focused on simplification. The existing webpage is cluttered and unintuitively laid out; most of the relevant information is obtained by navigating to other pages. The goal of this layout is to completely eliminate the clutter, reduce navigation fatigue and drive user applications. In order to do this, the vision was a simple homepage that is visually driven in which you are prompted to define your search by one of the two job categories.


Once the user selects one of the two options, the page will slide animate to reveal the Copy and Challenger statements with the full image backdrop. The content is cut down from the original site with only necessary information and short enough to draw someone in without being intimidating. Underneath the content block are two calls to action to drive job applications/inquiries. The tabular buttons opposite the text content on each respective page completely eliminate the need for a header nav bar and keep everything on one page to make navigating to other pertinent content simple. When clicked, each one of the three tabs will replace the text on the page to its respective content.

Visual Interface


The interface layout is designed to be sleek. As the “Ultimate Driving Machine” the BMW vehicle stands alone with a black backdrop, highlighting the vehicle without being obnoxious or in-your-face. In a more visually developed iteration, I would like the technician half of the landing page to be an x-ray of the car that shows the engine and the insides of the car. This imagery would aid the viewer in understanding the functions of each button as well as making the very simple page visually interesting. The buttons are designed to be understated, which we can manage without losing them because they are the only foreground items on the page. The slider, along with separating the images, has arrows on either side to indicate that the user can slide it back and forth to reveal more.


The content page interface clearly displays the text and the headline highlights the subject of the page with BMW’s blue. The call to action buttons are overlaid with a blue gradient so they catch the user’s eye since maximizing applications is the primary goal of this page. In the first iteration these buttons were the same style as the landing page buttons but were changed to be more obvious to the user.

Mobile Wireframes


The mobile wireframes are going off of the same ideas as the desktop wireframes with the slider animation removed to simplify development and maintain cleanliness. Instead of the slide animation, the screen will auto-scroll based on the user’s selected section. Additionally, I added a nav panel on the bottom right so the user can easily reach and navigate across the different sections. All desktop components were kept while maintaining the intuition and concept of the design.

Mobile Interface


The mobile interface is essentially a realignment of the content to fit the mobile layout in a comfortable and compatible way. Since the mobile backdrop is single image, the image was changed to be a top view of a BMW because this fits the dimensions more naturally. The tab bar at the bottom is designed with standard iOS layout.
